What is Bryce Dallas Howard’s Net Worth?

Bryce Dallas Howard, the daughter of filmmaker Ron Howard, has a net worth of $25 million.

She is best known for her roles in movies like “The Village,” “Spider-Man 3,” “The Help,” and “Jurassic World.”

In addition to acting, Howard directed the documentary “Dads” in 2019 and has directed episodes of “The Mandalorian” and “The Book of Boba Fett” on Disney+.

Bryce Dallas Howard’s Early Life and Education

Bryce Dallas Howard was born on March 2, 1981 in Los Angeles, California to filmmaker and actor Ron and writer Cheryl. She has two younger sisters, twins Paige and Jocelyn, as well as a younger brother named Reed. Howard and her siblings were raised away from the show business world and were encouraged to play outdoors and take up other hobbies.

At the age of seven, Howard was allowed to be an extra in films by her father. She appeared in several of his films, including “Parenthood,” “Apollo 13,” “How the Grinch Stole Christmas,” and “A Beautiful Mind.”

Howard was raised in Westchester County, New York and on a farm in Greenwich, Connecticut. She attended Stagedoor Manor performing arts camp and Greenwich Country Day School. She graduated from Byram Hills High School in 1999 and subsequently enrolled at New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts. Howard also took classes at the Stella Adler Studio of Acting and the Experimental Theatre Wing. She is an alumna of the Steppenwolf Theatre Company’s School in Chicago and the Actors Center in New York.

Bryce Dallas Howard’s Early Acting Career

Bryce Dallas Howard appeared in several theatrical productions in New York, such as “House & Garden,” “Tartuffe,” and Shakespeare’s “As You Like It.” She caught the attention of filmmaker M. Night Shyamalan, who cast her in his 2004 fantasy thriller “The Village,” where she played the lead female character Ivy. Howard then starred in Lars von Trier’s drama “Manderlay” and reunited with Shyamalan for the supernatural thriller “Lady in the Water.” She also reprised her stage role as Rosalind in Kenneth Branagh’s film adaptation of “As You Like It.”

Bryce Dallas Howard’s Acting Career Highlights

Bryce Dallas Howard rose to fame in 2007 with her role as Gwen Stacy in “Spider-Man 3.” She went on to star in the independent film “The Loss of a Teardrop Diamond” and the science-fiction action film “Terminator Salvation.” Howard had major roles in “The Twilight Saga: Eclipse” and “Hereafter” in 2010.

In 2011, Howard had significant supporting roles in “The Help” and “50/50.” She won the Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture for her role in “The Help.” Howard had one of her biggest roles yet in the blockbuster sequel “Jurassic World” in 2015.

Howard reprised her role in the sequels “Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom” and “Jurassic World: Dominion.” She also appeared in the crime drama “Gold” and portrayed the mother of Elton John in the musical biopic “Rocketman.”

Bryce Dallas Howard’s Career as Director and Actor

Howard directed a segment of the 2013 Lifetime anthology series “Call Me Crazy: A Five Film” and later gave an acclaimed performance in the “Nosedive” episode of “Black Mirror,” earning a Screen Actors Guild Award nomination. She went on to direct multiple episodes of “The Mandalorian” and “The Book of Boba Fett” on Disney+ in 2019 and 2020.

Personal Life

Bryce Dallas Howard met actor Seth Gabel while studying at New York University. The couple got married in June of 2006 and have two children together. Their first child, Theodore, was born only a week after their wedding. They later had a daughter named Beatrice in 2012. In 2019, Howard and her family moved from Santa Monica, California to an eco-conscious home in upstate New York that was previously owned by actor William Hurt.
