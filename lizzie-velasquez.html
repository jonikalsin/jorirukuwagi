Lizzie Velásquez Bio | Wiki

Lizzie Velásquez (Full Name: Elizabeth Anne Velásquez) is a popular American motivational speaker, activist, author, and YouTuber. She has also written two books directed at kids, which share personal stories as well as offer advice. Lizzie was born with an extremely rare congenital disease called Marfanoid–progeroid–lipodystrophy syndrome that, among other symptoms, prevents her from accumulating body fat and adding weight.

Lizzie’s conditions resulted in bullying during her childhood. In her teenage years, Lizzie faced cyberbullying, which ultimately inspired her to take up motivational speaking. Lizzie’s first work, co-authored with her mother, Rita, is a self-published autobiography published in 2010 in both English and Spanish. It is titled Lizzie Beautiful: The Lizzie Velásquez Story and includes letters her mother wrote to her as a child.

Lizzie Velasquez Syndrome

Her condition is a very rare, previously undiagnosed, and non-terminal genetic disorder. Lizzie’s condition bears similarities to many other conditions, especially progeria. Medical researchers at the Campus of Texas Southwestern Medical Center initially speculated that it may be a form of neonatal progeroid syndrome (NPS) (Wiedemann-Rautenstrauch syndrome), which does not affect her healthy bones, organs, and teeth.

Lizzie is medically unable to gain weight, which is a hallmark of her rare disorder. Lizzie has never weighed over 29 kg (64 lbs) and reportedly has almost 0% body fat. Additionally, Lizzie is required to eat many small meals and snacks throughout the day, averaging between 5,000 and 8,000 calories daily. Moreover, Lizzie is blind in her right eye, which started to cloud over when she was four, and she became vision-impaired in her left eye.

In 2015, it was disclosed that Lizzie and another woman named Abby Solomon, with the same but less severe variant of the condition, have mutations in the FBN1 gene, which encodes the proprotein of the novel hormone asprosin, and that this mutation results in asprosin deficiency and is responsible for their conditions. This condition is specifically named Marfanoid–progeroid–lipodystrophy syndrome or simply Marfan lipodystrophy syndrome.

Lizzie Velasquez Youtube

Ever since Lizzie was dubbed the “World’s Ugliest Woman” in a video posted on YouTube in 2006, when she was at the age of 17, Lizzie has spoken out against bullying. In January 2014 Lizzie gave a TEDxAustinWomen Talk titled “How Do YOU Define Yourself” and her YouTube videos have received more than 54 million views. Lizzie is noted for her optimism. In 2015, during National Bullying Prevention Month, Lizzie hosted a social media challenge for Bystander Revolution’s Month of Action.
