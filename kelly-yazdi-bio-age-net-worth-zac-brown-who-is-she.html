Kelly Yazdi Job

Kelly is the CEO of Ride Wild and an actress and model by trade. She stated on her LinkedIn that she has been serving in that role since 2017.

She is a natural-born explorer, promoter of events, SAG-AFTRA actress, stuntwoman, and model. Kelly’s obsession with speed began when she was a young child living in a rural Minnesota area and riding horses, dirt bikes, and snowmobiles.

She had a childhood surrounded by nature, going off-road with friends, and attempting anything that would allow her to travel faster and farther than on her own two feet. Kelly switched to riding motorcycles when she was 18 and aimed to inspire women and serve as a role model for living a passionate life.

Kelly founded the Ride Wild platform so that female Powersports riders could unite around a shared sense of tribe, adventure, and purpose. The original goals of Ride Wild were to change the perception of women in Powersports and to increase awareness of the drive, diversity, and spirit of women in motorsports.

Numerous women’s riding organizations (Biker Belles, The Litas, Iron Lilies, etc.), adventure outfitters, and businesses that share the same enthusiasm for fostering the riding community are partners with Ride Wild.

Kelly is also an accomplished public speaker and brand representative, due in part to her years of on-camera experience. Audiences may have seen Kelly on hit TV series such as “Hawaii Five-O” or “Steve Austin’s Broken Skull Ranch Challenge.” Her performances in movies like “The Martial Arts Kid,” and J.P.Getty Museum’s documentary ‘Herb Ritts: L.A. Style,” is also part of Kelly’s diverse on-screen portfolio.

She got her college education at UC Santa Barbara in International Relations and Affairs.
